# FMGE (Jan 2027) Roadmap

**Exam date:** January 9, 2027 (NBE-notified, CBT format)
**Today:** July 22, 2026 — approximately 24 weeks available
**Starting level:** Some background (foundational knowledge present, gaps span multiple subjects, not just the hard ones)
**Format:** 300 MCQs, no negative marking, pass = 150/300 (50% aggregate, no sectional cutoff)

## Why this sequencing

FMGE has no sectional cutoff — only the aggregate matters. That changes the optimal order: subjects are sequenced by **marks weight × how hard they are for you**, not by "easy subjects first." The four highest-weight subjects (Medicine 33, Surgery 32, PSM 30, OBG 30 — 125/300 marks combined) get priority regardless of tier. Luckily, three of those four (Surgery, PSM, OBG) are already in your easy tier, so they need revision-depth rather than first-principles depth. Medicine is both high-weight and hard for you, so it gets the most calendar time of any single subject.

Pre-clinical hard subjects (Anatomy, Physiology, Pathology, Pharmacology) come first because clinical subjects lean on them constantly — weak Anatomy makes Surgery/OBG harder to retain, weak Pathology makes Medicine harder to retain.

Low-weight subjects (Dermatology, Psychiatry, Radiodiagnosis, Radiotherapy, Anaesthesiology, Orthopaedics — 5 marks each, 30 marks combined) are batched into a single compressed phase late in the plan. Even if you nailed 100% of all six, that's only 30 marks — not worth early prime time.

## Phase 1 — Hard Pre-Clinical Foundations
**Jul 22 – Sep 1, 2026 (6 weeks)**

Subjects: Anatomy (17 marks), Physiology (17 marks), Pathology (13 marks), Pharmacology (13 marks)

- Week 1-2: Anatomy — regional anatomy (upper limb, lower limb, thorax, abdomen), high-yield embryology and neuroanatomy
- Week 3: Physiology — CVS, renal, GI, endocrine systems
- Week 4-5: Pathology — General pathology (inflammation, neoplasia, hemodynamics) then systemic pathology by organ system
- Week 6: Pharmacology — general pharmacology principles, ANS drugs, chemotherapy/antimicrobials
- Milestone: First full-length Part A mock (Anatomy/Physiology/Pathology/Pharm/Micro/Biochem/Forensic sections only)

## Phase 2 — Hard Clinical Core + Supporting Medium Subjects
**Sep 2 – Oct 13, 2026 (6 weeks)**

Subjects: General Medicine (33 marks, hard), Pediatrics (15 marks, hard), Microbiology (13 marks, medium), Biochemistry (17 marks, medium)

- Week 7-9: General Medicine — CVS, Respiratory, GI/Hepatology, Nephrology, Endocrine, Infectious Diseases, Neurology (this is your single highest-priority block — most marks + hardest tier)
- Week 10: Pediatrics — growth/development, neonatology, immunization, common pediatric emergencies
- Week 11: Microbiology — bacteriology high-yield organisms, virology, parasitology
- Week 12: Biochemistry — metabolism pathways, enzymology, vitamins, molecular biology basics
- Milestone: Subject-wise mock tests for Medicine and Pathology (your two weakest-scoring areas historically get re-tested here)

## Phase 3 — Easy High-Weight Clinical Subjects
**Oct 14 – Nov 17, 2026 (5 weeks)**

Subjects: Surgery (32 marks), OBG (30 marks), PSM (30 marks)

- Week 13-14: Surgery — general surgery principles, GI surgery, urology, surgical oncology, trauma
- Week 15-16: OBG — obstetrics (antenatal care, labor, complications), gynecology (menstrual disorders, infertility, gynec onc)
- Week 17: PSM — epidemiology, biostatistics, national health programs, nutrition
- Note: these are your easy tier — the goal here is fast, thorough revision and high accuracy, not first-principles relearning. If you're consistently scoring 80%+ in practice sets, compress this phase and pull Phase 4 forward.
- Milestone: Full-length Part B mock

## Phase 4 — Remaining Medium/Easy Subjects (Batched)
**Nov 18 – Dec 8, 2026 (3 weeks)**

Subjects: ENT (15), Ophthalmology (15), Forensic Medicine (10), Orthopaedics (5), Radiology — diagnosis + therapy (10 combined), Anaesthesia (5), Dermatology (5), Psychiatry (5)

- Week 18: ENT + Ophthalmology (30 marks combined, both easy tier — high-yield topic lists only)
- Week 19: Forensic Medicine + Orthopaedics (both easy/medium, low volume)
- Week 20: Radiology + Anaesthesia + Dermatology + Psychiatry (20 marks combined — treat as a single "minor subjects" sprint, focus only on classic high-yield facts, not exhaustive coverage)
- Milestone: Second full-length mock (all 300 questions, timed)

## Phase 5 — Full-Syllabus Grand Revision + Mock Test Cycle
**Dec 9 – Dec 29, 2026 (3 weeks)**

- Weighted revision: spend revision time proportional to marks (Medicine/Surgery/PSM/OBG get the most days; the 5-mark subjects get a single consolidated review day each)
- Take one full-length timed mock every 4-5 days (aim for 4-5 mocks this phase)
- After each mock: error-log review session — categorize misses as knowledge gap vs. careless vs. guessing, and feed weak spots back into short focused reviews
- Milestone: Score trend should be climbing toward 180+/300 by end of phase

## Phase 6 — Final Taper
**Dec 30, 2026 – Jan 8, 2027 (10 days)**

- Dec 30 – Jan 4: Rapid revision using your own short notes/flashcards only — no new material
- Jan 5 – Jan 6: One final full-length mock, then light review only
- Jan 7 – Jan 8: Light review, logistics check (admit card, exam center, ID), rest, sleep normalization
- Jan 9: Exam day

## How this stays current

This roadmap gets updated as we go — if a phase runs long or short, or your mock scores show a subject needs more/less time than planned, say so during a coach-me check-in and the plan (and tracker) will be adjusted.
